## 4. FAQ (Objection Handling)

**Q: How does Parse actually work?**  
A: Parse uses advanced AI models trained on millions of verified facts, cross-referenced with primary sources, academic databases, and expert fact-checking organizations. When you submit a URL or text, our system analyzes claims, detects bias, checks source credibility, and provides a confidence-scored report in seconds.

**Q: Is Parse biased itself?**  
A: We're transparent about our methodology. Parse doesn't take political positions—we measure bias against verified facts and disclose our confidence intervals. Our full methodology is published and audited quarterly by third-party researchers. You can see exactly how we score every claim.

**Q: What makes Parse different from Snopes or PolitiFact?**  
A: Traditional fact-checkers are manual and slow. Parse is automated and real-time. We analyze content the moment it's published, not days later. Plus, we don't just say "true" or "false"—we show you the full spectrum: bias, missing context, logical fallacies, and source reliability.

### Privacy & Security

**Q: What data do you collect?**  
A: We collect the URLs/text you analyze (to provide the service) and basic account info. We do NOT sell your data. We do NOT use your analysis history to train models without explicit opt-in. We do NOT share your reading habits with advertisers. Ever.

**Q: Is my data secure?**  
A: Yes. SOC 2 Type II certified. All data encrypted at rest and in transit. Enterprise customers can deploy on-premise for maximum control. We undergo annual third-party security audits.

**Q: Do you track what I read?**  
A: We only analyze what you explicitly submit to Parse. We don't monitor your browsing beyond the extension's scope (which you control). You can delete your history anytime.

## TONE GUIDELINES

**Professional**: No slang, no gimmicks, no false urgency  
**Trustworthy**: Cite sources, be transparent about limitations  
**Urgent**: Misinformation is a real threat—communicate stakes without fearmongering  
**Empowering**: Users can fight back, not helpless victims  
**Clear**: No jargon, no buzzwords, no corporate speak  

**Avoid**:
- "Revolutionary" / "Game-changing" (overused)
- Fear-based manipulation ("You're doomed without this")
- Overpromising ("100% accurate" — we're not)
- Competitor bashing (focus on our value, not their failures)

**Embrace**:
- Specificity (numbers, percentages, timeframes)
- Transparency (methodology, accuracy rates, limitations)
- Empathy (understand user frustration with misinformation)
- Action-orientation (clear next steps)
