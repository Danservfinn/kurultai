## The Landscape in 3 Sentences

The LLM evaluation market has 10+ players but zero clear winners in multi-agent orchestration. Braintrust ($249/mo) and LangSmith ($39/user) dominate single-LLM evaluation. HoneyHive is the only competitor explicitly targeting agents, but they're new (founded 2024) with custom-only pricing and a small community.

## Parse's Kill Zone: 3 Gaps No One Owns

| Gap | Why It's Open | Parse Move |
|-----|---------------|------------|
| **Multi-agent coordination** | Every competitor evaluates single LLMs or single agents. None trace cross-agent workflows. | Ship multi-agent tracing as the core differentiator in MVP. |
| **Agent lifecycle management** | HoneyHive does agent eval but not deployment. LangSmith deploys but only within LangChain. | Own the full build-test-deploy-monitor pipeline for any agent framework. |
| **Mid-market pricing** | $39/user (LangSmith) to $249/mo (Braintrust) leaves a dead zone. HoneyHive and Arize hide behind "contact sales." | $49-99/mo flat rate. Transparent. No sales call required. |

## 3 Risks to Name Out Loud

1. **LangSmith's ecosystem lock-in is a moat, not a weakness.** Teams already on LangChain won't switch. Parse must target framework-agnostic teams.
2. **Braintrust's unlimited free tier is aggressive.** 1M free spans vs our proposed 10K agent runs. We compete on agent-specificity, not volume.
3. **HoneyHive's $20K startup program** could steal early adopters. Consider a matching program.
